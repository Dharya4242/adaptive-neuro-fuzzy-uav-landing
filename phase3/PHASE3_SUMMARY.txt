🔬 WHAT EACH EXPERIMENT DOES
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Experiment 1: Ablation Study
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tests ANFIS with only 2 inputs:
  • Remove altitude  → use (velocity, wind)
  • Remove velocity  → use (altitude, wind)
  • Remove wind      → use (altitude, velocity)

Whichever removal causes the BIGGEST RMSE increase = most important feature.

Academic value: Proves all 3 inputs contribute meaningfully.


Experiment 2: Stress Testing
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Three extreme scenarios:
  1. Sudden gust      — calm → 12 m/s spike at t=10s
  2. Near-crash       — 8 m/s descent at 5m altitude
  3. High turbulence  — sustained 8-10 m/s wind

Shows which controller is more robust to real-world edge cases.

Academic value: Demonstrates safety under adversarial conditions.


Experiment 3: Statistical Analysis
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  • Paired t-test     → Is ANFIS improvement statistically significant?
  • Cohen's d         → HOW MUCH better is ANFIS? (effect size)
  • Bootstrap CI      → Are results stable across data splits?

If p < 0.05 and d > 0.8 → "significant with large effect" (publishable).

Academic value: Proves improvement isn't luck or overfitting.


Experiment 4: Real-Time Simulation
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Animated visualization showing:
  • Both drones landing side-by-side
  • Altitude, velocity, thrust over time
  • Wind disturbance (shared between both)
  • Final verdict: who landed safer & faster

Academic value: Makes results tangible and presentation-ready.
